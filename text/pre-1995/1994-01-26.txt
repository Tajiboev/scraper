RECALLS AND FIELD CORRECTIONS:  DEVICES -- CLASS I
========================
_______________
PRODUCT        Laederal Heartstart Semi-Automatic Defibrillators,portable
               battery powered units having an LCD monitor for displaying
               ECG and use audio and visual messages to guide the operator
               during use.  These units determine if the patient's
               condition can be treated by defibrillation.  The units can
               also be converted to manual operation:
               (a) HS 3000 - This is the basic unit as described above. 
               Each analysis of the patient has to be initiated by the
               operator;
               (b) HS 3000ATS - This model is essentially the same as the
               HS 3000 except that it will automatically reanalyze the
               patient after a shock is delivered for a series of 3 shocks. 
                (c) HS 3000QR - This is a HS 3000 unit with a Quick
               Reporter (QR) printing option added.  This option provides
               the ability to print out an event or patient ECG.  The
               operating instructions for this model would be those for the
               HS 3000 model and additional instructions for the HS 3000QR. 
               Recall #Z-722/724-3.
CODES          The following ranges of serial numbers for each model are
               involved, but the range of numbers is not all inclusive. 
               HS 3000:    6205 to 6648 
               HS 3000ATS: 1122 to 1171 
               HS 3000QR:  6213 to 6707 
               These are the serial numbers of units manufactured from the
               initial receipt of the suspect control boards (approximately
               9-92 to 3-3-93). 
MANUFACTURER   Siemens Electronics Malaysia, Penang, Malaysia (component).
RECALLED BY    Laerdal Manufacturing Corp. (LMC), Tualatin, Oregon, by
               letter May 10, 1993.  Firm-initiated recall ongoing.
DISTRIBUTION   Nationwide and Canada.
QUANTITY       545 units.
REASON         Potential for failure of the optocoupler because of its
               susceptibility to the effects of elevated temperature.  Such
               failure will render the device inoperable with a "check
               electrode" message that cannot be cleared.
